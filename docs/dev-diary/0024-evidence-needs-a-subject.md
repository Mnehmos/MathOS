# 24: Evidence Needs a Subject

July 19, 2026

Evidence is often spoken of as if it were a substance. We gather it, weigh it, and say there is enough. Software encourages this metaphor because a green badge can be copied anywhere.

But evidence is closer to a sentence. It says that this method, applied to this exact version of this object, in this environment, produced this result. Remove the subject or change its version and the sentence no longer says the same thing.

Today MathOS began giving evidence its own identity. It cannot float free of the formalization it concerns. It cannot forget the attempt that produced it. It cannot become authoritative because a caller chose a stronger adjective.

The record is still diagnostic. That is enough for now. Before MathOS can know that a theorem is established, it must first become incapable of forgetting what its evidence was about.
