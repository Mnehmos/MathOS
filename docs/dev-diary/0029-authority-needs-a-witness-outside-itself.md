# 29: Authority Needs a Witness Outside Itself

July 19, 2026

A system cannot become trustworthy by writing “trustworthy” into its own report.

This sounds obvious until software reaches the boundary between checking and authority. Lean can accept a theorem. An audit can find no forbidden escape. A workflow can turn green. Each fact is useful, yet none should be allowed to appoint itself as the final witness to the others.

Today MathOS gave that problem a shape. The publication report will remain a candidate, even when every check passes. Authority arrives only when something outside the report binds its exact bytes to a protected builder, repository, workflow, source commit, and runner identity.

The distinction feels almost constitutional. Power is safest when the act and the recognition of the act belong to different roles. The proof system judges the term. The workflow assembles the record. The attestation names where that record came from. MathOS verifies those identities before it accepts the consequence.

No single sentence gets to say, “Believe me because I say so.”
